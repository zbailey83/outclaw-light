# Cold DM + Reverse Cold Email — Outreach That Actually Gets Replies

Two outreach strategies that work because they lead with value, not pitch.

---

## Strategy 1: Real-Time Problem Monitoring

Find people actively complaining about problems Outclaw solves — right now, today.

### Setup: Google Alerts
Create alerts for:
- "AI tools overwhelmed"
- "which AI tool should I use"
- "AI newsletter recommendation"
- "AI tool that actually works"
- "[competitor newsletter name]"

### Setup: Twitter/X Monitoring
Search (and save as a Twitter search or use a tool like TweetDeck) for:
- "looking for AI tools newsletter"
- "AI tool recommendations"
- "[competitor] is bad"
- "anyone know a good AI newsletter"
- "overwhelmed by AI tools"

### The Speed Rule
The first helpful reply wins. Reply within minutes, not hours. When someone tweets "ugh I can't figure out which AI tool to use for X" — if you're the first helpful response, you own that conversation.

**Don't send a link first.** Start a conversation. Ask what they need. Understand their situation. Then offer Outclaw when it's genuinely the right fit.

---

## Strategy 2: The Reverse Cold Email

Response rates go from 2% to 15–20% using this method.

### Who to Target
- Founders or marketers at companies that match Outclaw's ideal reader profile
- Bloggers or content creators in the AI/productivity/marketing space
- People who write about AI but could benefit from Outclaw's depth

Find them via:
- LinkedIn search: "content marketing" + "AI" + [company size filter]
- Twitter: follow who your best readers follow
- ProductHunt: look at who's commenting on AI tool launches

### The Email Structure

**Email 1 (the hook — value only, no pitch):**
> Subject: quick thing I noticed about [their site/content]
>
> Hey [name],
>
> Saw your piece on [specific thing]. Quick observation that might be useful: [1-sentence insight about their content/site/approach].
>
> Happy to share more if it'd be helpful.
>
> [Name]

**Wait for reply. Do NOT pitch Outclaw here.**

**Email 2 (after they respond — deliver the value):**
> [Deliver the insight/resource/tip you promised]
>
> BTW — I run a newsletter called Outclaw that covers AI tools for practitioners. We do hands-on testing and honest takes — no hype. Might be relevant depending on what you're working on. Happy to send you an issue if you want a look.

### What "Value" Looks Like
- A broken link on their site
- An insight about how a competitor is approaching a topic they cover
- A data point that's relevant to something they recently wrote
- A tool or resource they'd genuinely find useful

**The rule:** The value must be real. Don't manufacture it. If you can't find something genuinely useful to share, move to the next prospect.

---

## The Follow-Up Protocol

For both DMs and emails:
- Follow up once, 3 days after first contact
- Message: "Hey — just checking in on [what you offered/shared]. Did it end up being useful?"
- If no reply after follow-up, move on. No third contact.

## Tracking

Keep a simple spreadsheet:
| Name | Platform | Contact Date | Status | Follow-up Date | Notes |
|------|----------|--------------|--------|----------------|-------|

Statuses: Contacted / Replied / Subscribed / Not Interested / No Response

Review weekly. The pipeline is only valuable if you follow up.
