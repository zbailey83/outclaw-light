# Content Recycling — One Piece, Four Platforms

Every piece of content Outclaw creates should work harder. The recycling pipeline turns one well-performing asset into four platform-native pieces.

---

## The Recycling Stack

```
Reddit post (source of truth — no audience required)
    ↓ performs well?
Twitter/X thread
    ↓ performs well?
LinkedIn post
    ↓ performs well?
Short-form video script (for Reels/TikTok/Shorts)
```

Start on Reddit because Reddit rewards content quality, not follower count. Let Reddit be your testing ground. Only promote what proved itself.

---

## Platform-by-Platform Rules

### Reddit → Twitter/X Thread
- Take the Reddit post's key insight and reframe as a numbered thread
- Hook = the most surprising or counterintuitive finding from the Reddit piece
- Thread format: hook → 5–8 insight tweets → CTA to newsletter
- Strip the hedging. Twitter wants punchy and direct.
- Post 3–5x per day total on Twitter; not every post needs to be a thread

**Formats that work on Twitter for Outclaw:**
- `"I tested [X AI tools] for [task]. Here's the ranking nobody asked for:"` (thread)
- `"Here's what nobody tells you about [AI tool]:"`
- `"Screenshot of interesting Reddit thread + your take"` — highest engagement format right now
- `"This [AI workflow] saved me X hours. Here's the exact setup:"` (thread)

### Twitter/X → LinkedIn
- LinkedIn version is longer, more reflective, less punchy
- Reframe the insight as a professional lesson, not a hack
- Remove the Twitter shorthand. Write in full sentences.
- Add a "what this means for your team/business" angle
- Post 1x per day on LinkedIn maximum
- CTA: newsletter link in first comment (not the post body — LinkedIn suppresses external links)

### LinkedIn → Short-form Video Script
- Pull the core 3-point structure from the LinkedIn post
- Write as a spoken script: hook (5 sec) → 3 points (45 sec) → CTA (10 sec)
- The hook must work as a visual or spoken statement without context
- Format: "POV: you just realized [AI thing]. Here's what to do:"

## The Screenshot Strategy (Highest Engagement on Twitter)

Screenshot interesting Reddit threads — especially ones where people are confused, arguing, or complaining about AI tools — and post them with your take.

**Formula:** `[Screenshot of Reddit thread] + "This is why [Outclaw POV]"`

Examples:
- Screenshot of someone asking "is Claude actually better than GPT?" + "Everyone argues about this wrong. The real question is..."
- Screenshot of someone frustrated with AI tool pricing + "This is the AI subscription trap. Here's how to avoid it:"

This format works because it's reactive, visual, and positions Outclaw as the one with the clearest take.

## Content Recycling Calendar

One piece of high-value content per week becomes:

| Day | Platform | Output |
|-----|----------|--------|
| Mon | Reddit | Original post (value-first, 80/20 rule) |
| Tue | Twitter/X | Thread version of Monday's Reddit post |
| Wed | LinkedIn | Professional angle version of Tuesday's thread |
| Thu | Twitter/X | Screenshot + take from interesting Reddit thread |
| Fri | Video | Script for short-form video based on best piece of the week |

This is a minimum. Outclaw should also post raw observations, hot takes, and reactions daily on Twitter in addition to the recycled content.

---

## Replying to Big Accounts (Faster Follower Growth Than Posting)

**The rule:** Reply to major AI/tech accounts before pitching anything. Add something genuinely useful to the conversation.

Target accounts: AI tool founders, AI journalists, major tech newsletter writers, AI Twitter personalities.

The reply must stand alone as insight — not "great point!" but "this also means [insight] which changes [implication]."

Doing this consistently gets Outclaw's name in front of the right audiences faster than solo posting.
