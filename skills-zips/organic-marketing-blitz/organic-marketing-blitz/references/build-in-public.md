# Build in Public + Daily Twitter Posting

Building in public is not a strategy. It's THE strategy. The audience you build while building Outclaw becomes the distribution channel for Outclaw.

---

## The Core Principle

Put your face on everything. Profile pic. Videos. Screenshots with your face in the corner. People trust people, not logos. Nobody wants to follow a faceless SaaS account or an AI-generated brand. They want to follow the person behind it.

**Consistency beats quality. A mid post every day beats a perfect post once a week.**

---

## What to Post Daily on Twitter

Post raw, unfiltered, real. Not polished marketing copy. Real moments:

**Failures:**
- "I tried [X] today and it completely failed. Here's what I learned:"
- "Lost a subscriber today. Asked them why. Their answer changed how I think about [thing]."
- "Wasted 3 hours on [AI tool]. Here's what went wrong and what I'd do differently:"

**Wins:**
- "Just hit [milestone]. Took me [time]. Here's what actually moved the needle:"
- "First [X] subscribers. The thing that made the difference wasn't [obvious thing]."

**Lessons:**
- "Here's what nobody told me about running an AI tools newsletter:"
- "3 months in. What I got completely wrong about [topic]:"
- "The AI tool I was most skeptical about ended up being the most useful. Here's why:"

**Observations:**
- "Something I keep seeing in AI tool releases that frustrates me:"
- "The gap between AI tool demos and AI tool reality is getting wider. Example:"
- "Hot take: [AI thing] is overhyped because [specific reason with evidence]"

---

## Daily Posting Cadence

**Twitter/X: 3–5 posts per day**
- 1 original insight or observation
- 1 recycled/repurposed piece (from Reddit or LinkedIn)
- 1 reply to a big account in the AI/tech space
- 1 screenshot of something interesting + your take
- (Optional) 1 thread if you have something with depth to say

**LinkedIn: 1 post per day**
- Professional, reflective version of the week's best Twitter content
- External link in first comment, not the post body

---

## The White-Glove Early Subscriber Experience

Your first 100 subscribers should feel like they know you personally. This doesn't scale — that's the point.

**For every early subscriber:**
1. Manually send a personal welcome email from your real email address (not a no-reply)
2. Ask what they're trying to do with AI tools — what's the specific problem they're trying to solve?
3. Help them with that specific problem in the reply
4. Follow up 3 days later: "Hey, did [thing we talked about] work out?"

**Why this works:**
These users become evangelists. They tell colleagues. They leave reviews. They post about Outclaw. They're not just subscribers — they're your distribution network.

**The email template:**
> Subject: Hey [name] — quick question
>
> Hey [name],
>
> I'm the person who runs Outclaw. Genuinely happy you're here.
>
> Quick question: what made you subscribe? What's the AI tools problem you're trying to solve?
>
> I ask because I want to make sure Outclaw's actually useful to you specifically — not just generally.
>
> [Your name]

## Build-in-Public Content Calendar Integration

The "build in public" content feeds the newsletter and vice versa:
- Things you learn building Outclaw → Twitter thread → Newsletter deep-dive
- Newsletter research → interesting finding → Twitter observation → Reddit post
- Subscriber feedback → "here's what my readers keep asking about" post

Every channel reinforces the others. The build-in-public content is what makes Outclaw feel human and trustworthy vs. every other AI newsletter that feels generated and hollow.
