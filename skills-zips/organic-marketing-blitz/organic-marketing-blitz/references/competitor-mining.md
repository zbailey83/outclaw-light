# Competitor Review Mining — Warmest Leads Available

People who leave 1-star reviews on competitors already pay for a solution, already hate it, and already told you exactly what they want. They are the warmest leads that exist.

---

## Where to Mine

- **G2** — Best for SaaS-adjacent AI tools
- **Capterra** — Business software users
- **Trustpilot** — Consumer and prosumer tools
- **Product Hunt** — Look at negative comments on competitor launches
- **Reddit** — Search "[competitor name] sucks" or "[competitor name] problems" in relevant subreddits
- **Twitter/X** — Monitor for "[competitor name] is broken" or "frustrated with [competitor]"

---

## Outclaw-Specific Competitors to Mine

Mine reviews for every major AI newsletter and AI tools aggregator:
- The Rundown AI
- TLDR AI
- Superhuman (newsletter)
- Ben's Bites
- Every.to
- AI Tool Report
- Futurepedia (tools directory)
- There's An AI For That

Also mine reviews for tools Outclaw covers. If people are frustrated with Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Jasper, etc., they need exactly what Outclaw provides: honest guidance on what actually works.

---

## The Mining Process

**Step 1: Screenshot the reviews**
Filter for 1-star and 2-star reviews. Screenshot ones where the person describes a pain point Outclaw directly solves:
- "I don't know which AI tool to use"
- "Too much noise, not enough signal"
- "I waste so much time reading AI content that goes nowhere"
- "The newsletter just summarizes headlines, no real insight"
- "I need to know what actually works, not what sounds impressive"

**Step 2: Find them**
- Search their name on LinkedIn and Twitter
- If they left a G2 review, they often have a LinkedIn profile linked

**Step 3: The outreach**

Don't pitch. Open a conversation.

**LinkedIn DM:**
> "Hey [name] — saw your review of [competitor]. Sounds like [specific frustration they mentioned] has been a real pain. Curious — did you end up finding something that worked better, or still looking?"

**Twitter DM:**
> "Saw your note about [competitor] — [specific frustration]. Same thing frustrated me. Did you find a fix or still dealing with it?"

**The follow:**
Half the time they'll ask what you're building. Now you have a conversation, not a cold pitch.

When they ask: "I run Outclaw — it's a newsletter for AI tools practitioners. We actually test the tools and give you honest takes on what's worth your time vs. what's hype. Happy to share an issue if you want to see if it's relevant."

---

## The Failure Mode

Sending a pitch immediately. "Saw your review of X, you should try Outclaw!" reads as spam and gets ignored. The opener is a genuine question about their experience. Let the conversation evolve.

---

## Volume and Cadence

- Mine 2–3 competitor review pages per week
- Target 5–10 outreach DMs per week from this channel specifically
- Track who responds in a simple spreadsheet: name, platform, status, follow-up date
- Follow up once after 3 days: "Hey, just checking — did you end up sorting out [problem]?"
