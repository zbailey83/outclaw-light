# Community Infiltration — Slack, Discord, Facebook, and Niche Forums

One good community drives 20–30 subscribers on its own. Most people join and immediately promote. That's the failure mode. The playbook is to lurk first, help second, mention Outclaw third.

---

- Specific AI tool community forums

## The Infiltration Protocol

### Week 1–2: Lurk
- Join 5–10 communities
- Read threads. Don't post anything.
- Note: What questions come up repeatedly? What do people struggle with? What tools do they ask about?
- This intelligence is also content research for Outclaw posts.

### Week 3+: Help
- Answer questions where you have genuine expertise
- Share useful resources (not Outclaw — third-party resources)
- Become a known name by being consistently helpful
- Engage with other people's posts: add context, ask good follow-up questions

### When It's Right: Mention Outclaw
- After 2–3 weeks of being helpful, mention Outclaw only when it's directly relevant
- Don't say "subscribe to my newsletter." Say "I covered this exact thing in Outclaw last week — [link to the specific article]."
- Many communities have a dedicated "share your project" or "self-promo" channel. Use it once per week maximum.

---

## What Outclaw Should Share in Communities

When it IS appropriate to share Outclaw:
- Link to a specific article that answers a question someone just asked
- Share a free tool or resource from Outclaw that's relevant to the thread
- Post a newsletter issue summary in a "share what you're reading" thread
- Invite people to the Outclaw subreddit (if it's been built)

**Never:** "Check out my newsletter." That's noise.
**Always:** "Someone asked about [specific thing] — we just published a breakdown of exactly this: [link]."

---

## Community Intelligence → Content Pipeline

Every community you're in is a content research goldmine:
- Screenshot questions that come up repeatedly → write a post answering that question
- Note which AI tools people keep arguing about → write a comparison
- Note what people say is missing from existing newsletters → write that content for Outclaw

The communities aren't just distribution — they're your editorial calendar.
