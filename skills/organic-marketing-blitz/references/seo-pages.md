# SEO Pages — Comparison, Alternatives, and Boring Blog Posts

Two types of SEO content that punch above their weight for Outclaw:
1. **Comparison and alternative pages** — Highest buying intent, fastest to rank
2. **Problem-answer blog posts** — Boring to write, compound for years

---

## Part 1: Comparison & Alternative Pages

### Why These Work
People search "[tool] alternative" or "[tool A] vs [tool B]" right before they make a buying or subscription decision. Every visitor has buying intent. These pages are the highest-ROI SEO you can do.

### Pages Outclaw Should Build First

**Alternative pages:**
- "The Rundown AI Alternative" → Outclaw
- "TLDR AI Alternative" → Outclaw
- "Ben's Bites Alternative" → Outclaw
- "Futurepedia Alternative" → Outclaw
- "ChatGPT alternative to [specific use case]"
- "Jasper AI alternative"
- "Copy.ai alternative"

**Comparison pages:**
- "Outclaw vs The Rundown AI"
- "Outclaw vs TLDR AI"
- "Claude vs ChatGPT for [specific workflow]"
- "Jasper vs Copy.ai vs [third option]"
- "Best AI writing tools compared"

### How to Write These Pages

**Structure:**
1. Intro: who this comparison is for (be specific — "if you're a marketer using AI to write copy")
2. TLDR comparison table (feature, pricing, best for)
3. Section on Product A — what it's good at, what it's bad at (be honest)
4. Section on Product B — same
5. Our verdict — who should pick which one
6. CTA: "We cover stuff like this in Outclaw every week. [Subscribe link]"

**Rules:**
- Don't trash the competitor. Show where you're different.
- Include pricing breakdowns (people always want to know what things cost)
- Include screenshots of actual interfaces or outputs
- Update monthly — fresh data keeps rankings
- Link internally to relevant Outclaw tutorials and reviews

### The Failure Mode
Writing biased comparisons that read like ads for Outclaw. Readers spot this instantly and bounce. The honest weakness column is what makes the page trustworthy and what keeps people reading.

## Part 2: Boring Blog Posts (The Compound Traffic Machine)

### The Strategy
Find the exact questions your target customers are Googling. Write long-form posts answering those questions in detail. Don't mention Outclaw until the last paragraph. Post one per week for 6 months. Watch traffic compound.

### How to Find Questions

**Google method:**
1. Type your niche topic into Google
2. Look at "People also ask" section
3. Look at "Related searches" at the bottom
4. Those are your post topics

**AnswerThePublic / AlsoAsked:**
Run searches for "AI tools", "Claude AI", "ChatGPT for [workflow]", "best AI tools for [use case]"

**Outclaw-specific topic vein:**
- "How to use Claude for [specific task]"
- "How to set up [AI workflow]"
- "Best AI tools for [specific job role]"
- "How to [task] without hiring a copywriter"
- "Is [AI tool] worth it for [use case]"
- "How to fix [common AI tool problem]"
- "What's the difference between [AI model A] and [AI model B]"

### How to Write These Posts

**Structure:**
1. Title = the exact question (verbatim from Google's "People also ask")
2. TLDR: answer the question in 2–3 sentences at the top
3. Main content: step-by-step, specific, with real examples
4. Don't mention Outclaw until the final section
5. Final section: "We cover AI tool workflows like this every week in Outclaw — [subscribe CTA]"

**The key rule:** The post should be genuinely useful on its own. If the reader never subscribes, the post still helped them. That's what earns trust, and trust is what converts.

### Weekly SEO Cadence

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Publish 1 comparison page (highest-priority competitor) |
| 2 | Publish 1 alternative page |
| 3 | Publish 1 boring blog post (answer a "how to" question) |
| 4 | Update the comparison page with fresh pricing/data |
| Ongoing | 1 new boring blog post per week |

---

## Internal Linking Rule

Every new page should link to at least 2 existing Outclaw pages, and every existing relevant page should get a link added back to the new one. Don't let pages become orphans. The SEO value of a well-interlinked site compounds faster than any individual page.
